Work Experience

Developers Institute

After two years of being a stay at home mum, and with many people losing their jobs with the impact of COVID lockdowns, I found it almost impossible to find job opportunities. I put all my efforts into my studies with Developers Institute and in the end, was offered a position as an Associate Developer. My role is split into two parts Quality Assurance and Learner Wellbeing. For Quality Assurance my role would be working with NZQA to ensure that we provided well-rounded material that reflects the needs of potential employers for graduates, working through code projects to ensure that they are graded fairly and accurately and overall as a business ensuring that we learners are achieving as expected. As the learner wellbeing support, I would aid learners with different tools they could use to help them code, work through exercises, hold one on one sessions if they needed help going over materials and discussing their work-life balance. As part of the Developers Institute team, I have been able to work on different projects including updating our website using contentful, collaborating on projects to share with learners with our latest project to build software that supports online education to combine several platforms we use into one interface to collate data. We use Agile work methods in a high trust environment, where we collaborate using the shape up method to work on projects. We utilise tools such as Jira and Confluence to document and visualise tasks that need to be done. I have already grown in so many ways with Developers Institute where again I have started to gain confidence not only in my role but my influence.

Call Centre

After moving to Auckland, I started in a call centre in downtown Auckland for Tower Insurance. This was the change I needed to challenge myself while building on the skillset I had. I started in customer service and worked my way up to being a part of the sales team. I have always liked to continuously learn and grow, so after a few years, I decided to explore other parts of the business. I worked with the Business insurance team, retention, and TradeMe team until I found claims. Moving to claims was a hugely rewarding change, where I was dealing with motor claims for boats, bikes and personal use vehicles. I would assist customers as well as businesses, manage financial responsibilities and be able to context switch between several claims at one time. At the end of 6 months, It was harder to leave work at work as dealing with severe incidents that our customers were involved with began to take a toll. I left to join the Department of Internal Affairs with the passport team. I would often deal with passport applications for customers, help the team reach out to customers who needed information and handle calls for other teams as well. It was during this time that I fell pregnant and left the corporate offices behind me to become a full-time mum.

McDonald's

I started working at McDonald’s as an after school job to pay for things I wanted to do with my friends but as I came to the end of school, I wanted to stay in Taranaki so decided to stay and worked my way to become the youngest manager in the store. At 17 and 18 I was managing a staff team of 13 most nights, handling customer complaints, sales targets, stock management and the running of the store. Before each shift, I would make a roster of where our crew members would be set up, list our sale target that we needed to meet for each hour, ensure we had enough stock for the shift and plan how our team would work together. During the night, it was important to engage with the team to see how things were running and that we had not only satisfied customers but staff who were able to take breaks in order to perform well. This experience taught me about my own capabilities as I started to gain more confidence in my role and undertake new responsibilities. I decided that I couldn’t grow any more in my position and after 7 years of dedication, I decided there was more outside our town and moved to Auckland.
